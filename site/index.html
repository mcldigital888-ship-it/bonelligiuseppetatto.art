The artist

One-of-one pieces, drawn from the story they carry.

Giuseppe Bonelli is an Italian realism tattoo artist specializing in hyper-realistic black & grey and color tattoos — portraits, wildlife, sacred and religious realism, and large-scale architectural sleeves. He tattoos professional footballers including Lautaro Martínez, Gianluigi Donnarumma and Alessio Romagnoli, and tours U.S. tattoo conventions in 2026.

For 16 years he has refined photorealism in skin — studying how light falls across a face, fur and stone the way a painter studies a subject. Every piece is a one-of-one commission, built in deep contrast and fine gradient so it reads like a photograph and ages like one.

His work is worn by champions: the lion across the back of Lautaro Martínez (Inter Milan & Argentina), plus pieces for footballers Gianluigi Donnarumma and Alessio Romagnoli.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

How do I book a tattoo with Giuseppe Bonelli?

Send a request through the booking form with your idea, placement, size and a reference image. Giuseppe reviews every enquiry personally and replies within 3–5 days. If your project is a fit, you'll receive available dates and a deposit link to lock in your appointment — at the studio or on the U.S. convention tour.

How much does a realism tattoo cost?

Pricing depends on size, placement, detail and whether it's black & grey or color. Giuseppe works at a day rate for large pieces and a session minimum for smaller work; an exact quote is given after he sees your idea and reference. As a guide, full-day realism sessions in the U.S. typically start around $1,200–$1,800.

Do you require a deposit?

Yes. A non-refundable deposit confirms any appointment and is applied to the final cost of your tattoo. It reserves your slot and covers design time, and is forfeited for no-shows or cancellations with less than 72 hours' notice.

What styles do you specialize in?

Photorealistic black & grey realism (portraits, religious and sacred figures, statues), color realism, animal and wildlife realism, and large-scale sleeves and back pieces including architectural realism. Every piece is custom-designed one-to-one — no flash, no copies.

Do you tattoo in the United States?

Yes. Giuseppe tattoos across the U.S. on the 2026 convention tour, with stops in Portland, New Hampshire, Houston, Virginia Beach, Nashville and Jacksonville. Reserve a tour slot through the booking form — convention spots are limited.

How long does a realism piece take?

Small pieces take a single session of a few hours. Portraits and medium work usually need one full day. Sleeves, back pieces and large-scale realism are built over multiple sessions, spaced a few weeks apart to let the skin heal between sittings.

Does it hurt, and can I use numbing cream?

Realism involves long, detailed sessions, so some discomfort is normal — placement matters more than style. A topical numbing cream is welcome; let Giuseppe know in advance so the session can be planned around it.

Can you cover up or rework an old tattoo?

Cover-ups and reworks are considered case by case and depend on the existing tattoo's size, color and age. Send a clear photo with your enquiry and Giuseppe will tell you honestly whether realism is the right solution.

Do you offer free touch-ups?

Yes — one complimentary touch-up within 90 days for tattoos that need minor adjustment after healing, provided aftercare was followed. Touch-ups outside that window are charged at the session minimum.

What's the minimum age to get tattooed?

You must be 18 or older with a valid government-issued photo ID. No exceptions, even with parental consent.

Aftercare

How to heal your realism tattoo.

Good healing is part of the work. Realism lives on fine contrast and soft gradients — how you care for it in the first month decides how sharp it stays for years.

First 24 hours

Leave the covering on for the time Giuseppe tells you. Wash your hands, remove it gently, wash with lukewarm water and fragrance-free soap, and pat dry with a clean paper towel — never rub.

Days 1–3

Wash 2–3 times a day and apply a thin layer of fragrance-free balm — less is more. Some plasma, ink and redness is normal.

Days 4–14

Flaking and light scabbing begin. Do not pick, scratch or peel — that pulls ink out and is the #1 cause of patchy realism. It may look cloudy as it heals; this is temporary.

Weeks 3–6

The surface heals while the skin settles underneath. Keep it moisturized, and once healed always use SPF 30+ — sun fades black & grey contrast and color fastest.

Avoid while healing

Direct sun, tanning beds, pools, the sea, hot tubs, saunas, soaking baths, gym contact and tight clothing over the tattoo.

When to get help

If you see spreading redness, swelling, heat, pus or fever, contact a doctor — and let Giuseppe know. One free touch-up within 90 days if a healed area needs minor work.
